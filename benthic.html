'Benthic' Mural

Ink, 2nd floor of Ryder Hall, Northeastern University

'Benthic' is an ink mural on the second floor of Ryder Hall, in Northeastern University that I completed in April, 2015. Even though the mural started as a final for my Conceptual Drawing course, it eventually took on a life of its own, and remains in Ryder to this day.

Besides a few previously drawn designs that I wanted to include, everything was drawn freehand in ink. I started in the bottom left corner, and worked on it for a few hours every night for two and a half weeks.

The term "benthic" refers to the class of life that lives on the bottom of a body of water. I used to be a biology major before switching to my current design major, but I still draw inspiration from biological and chemical concepts. The work, though abstract, speaks to the diversity of life in the ocean, and the interwoven systems that exist and function in harmony.
